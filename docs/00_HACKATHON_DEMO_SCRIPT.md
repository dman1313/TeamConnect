# Demo and Presentation Specification

This document details the intended flow and narrative presentation of AGORA Agentic Goal-Oriented Reflective Adaptive academy during the hackathon demonstration.

## Narrative Hook
The presentation begins by establishing the core vision: delivering top-tier, personalized education to individuals globally, regardless of their geographical location or financial budget. The emphasis is placed on replacing traditional instruction with an agentic "Cognitive Coach" that empowers users rather than lecturing them.

## Demonstration Phases

### 1. Goal Setting via Cognitive Coaching
The demonstration showcases the initial onboarding and the first interaction with the agent. The presenter highlights the agent's unique behavior: refusing to give direct answers, and instead utilizing a loop of paraphrasing user input, pausing, and asking open-ended questions. 
- **[Missing] Multi-User Alignment Demo:** The demonstration should include a scenario showing a small group (3 users) required to reach 100% alignment on the formulated goal via a real-time dashboard before the system allows them to proceed.

### 2. Success Criteria Definition
The presentation moves to defining the success criteria, adhering strictly to the Brené Brown methodology of defining "What does done look like?". The presenter demonstrates the system's strict enforcement of binary (Yes/No) criteria.
- **[Missing] Proof Verification System:** The demo highlights a proposed addition where users explicitly prove their "Yes" criteria by uploading photographic evidence, which is then verified by the platform.

### 3. Dynamic RAG and Curation
The presenter shows the ingestion of learning materials into the platform. The agent dynamically adjusts the complexity of the material based on the group's current state (e.g., generating a high-level summary versus a deep-dive reading assignment).
- **[Missing] Real-Time Pedagogical Generation:** The demo shows the system instantly generating a set of flashcards from the ingested material to demonstrate the platform's utility as an active study tool.

### 4. Gamification and Empathy Recovery
The final phase of the demonstration focuses on the platform's retention mechanics. The presenter shows the daily action loop, demonstrating standard token rewards for successful task completion.
- **[Missing] The Empathy Loop:** A critical moment in the demo where a simulated user fails an action. Instead of penalizing the user, the system detects the struggle, lowers the difficulty of the next task, and awards "Recovery Tokens" to boost morale and prevent platform abandonment.
